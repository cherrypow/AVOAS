Packs of dogs roam as if in echoes of spirit, blood dripping from their jaws. They slink with wet coats, shivering through the storm. You hide beneath a tree. You hide beneath a tree. Deep somewhere, somewhere not here or there is a birthing of a picture. She comes out of a lake vibrating in the rain, her body is pale and burning and beating is her heart and her eyes are black and alien thoughts move like storms behind them. She is dripping and steaming and flickering in light. Tell them she says do not call me, do not speak. A crack of lightning calls from out of her throat and whelms you, she falls to her knees and takes the knife against her neck and the hot blood like perfume for the dogs to follow and she is burning and you can't forget her. Close to her face, she whispers, paint me, before it's all over. Before everything ends, the thing you want you can't have. Then the rain, dripping down lampposts, the sound with the window open. The folding breaks of thunder like sonorous shores aspiring to still themselves to silence. The sky closed, covered in her dark hair, her hair falling in lobes against his face. Closing his eyes. Remember me? Can't you see me through the storm? Lost colours. My eyes are fireblue. My body is fresh and hot as campfire. Remember me watching you?
